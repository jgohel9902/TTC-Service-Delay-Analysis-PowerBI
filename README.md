🚍 TTC Service Delay Analysis (Power BI Dashboard)

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes Toronto Transit Commission (TTC) service delay data using Power BI.

The dashboard is designed to identify:
- High-risk routes
- Operational delay hotspots
- Directional delay patterns
- Monthly delay trends
- Route performance movement over time

The objective is to transform raw operational data into actionable transportation intelligence.

## 📊 Dashboard Structure

🔴 Page 1 – Executive Overview
- TTC Service Delay Analysis
- Total Delay Minutes
- Total Incidents
- Average Delay Minutes
- % Incidents With Delay
- Top 10 Routes by Total Delay Impact
- Directional Delay Distribution
- Monthly Delay Trend
This page provides a high-level operational summary for decision makers.


🔴 Page 2 – Route Performance Quadrant
- Route Performance Quadrant
- Route Risk Classification (Incidents vs Avg Delay)
- Top 10 Routes by Incident Count
- Top 10 Routes by Average Delay Severity
- Route Rank Movement Over Time (Ribbon Chart)
This page highlights high-risk routes and performance volatility.

🔴 Page 3 – Geospatial Intelligence
- TTC Operational Hotspots
- 3D Geospatial Delay Impact Map (Azure Maps)
- Delay Contribution by Direction (Daily Distribution)
- Average Delay Severity by Direction and Day
This page identifies spatial delay clusters and operational pressure zones across Toronto.


## 🛠 Tools & Technologies
- Microsoft Power BI
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- Azure Maps (3D & Heatmap Layer)
- Ribbon Chart
- Scatter Quadrant Analysis
- Advanced KPI Cards
-Custom TTC Branding Theme

## 📈 Key Insights Identified
- Certain routes contribute disproportionately to total delay minutes.
- Delay severity varies significantly across route directions.
- Seasonal patterns show delay spikes mid-year and in December.
-High-incident routes are not always the highest delay-severity routes.
- Geospatial clustering reveals operational congestion corridors.
